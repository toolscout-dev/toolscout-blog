# Cal.com vs Calendly: Why the Open Source Alternative Wins in 2026

Calendly owns the scheduling market. But in 2026, a challenger with 40,000+ GitHub stars is eating its lunch—offering unlimited free scheduling, full data control, and pricing that undercuts Calendly at every tier.

Meet **Cal.com**—the open source Calendly successor.

## What Is Cal.com?

Cal.com is an open-source scheduling platform founded by Peer Richelsen and Bailey Pumfleet. It does everything Calendly does—calendar connections, booking pages, team scheduling—but with a critical difference: you control your data, your branding, and your infrastructure.

The platform has exploded in popularity among privacy-conscious teams, developers, and enterprises tired of SaaS lock-in. With SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA, GDPR, and ISO 27001 compliance, it's not just for indie hackers anymore.

## The Pricing Reality Check

| Plan | Cal.com | Calendly |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Free** | Unlimited event types, 100+ integrations | 1 event type, limited features |
| **Teams** | $12/user/month | $16/user/month |
| **Enterprise** | $28/user/month | $20k+/year (custom) |

**The free tier alone destroys Calendly.** Unlimited event types, unlimited calendars, payment collection via Stripe/PayPal, and 100+ app integrations. Calendly's free plan gives you one event type and basic scheduling—barely functional for serious use.

At the team level, Cal.com saves you $4 per user per month. For a 20-person team, that's $960 annually—real money for startups.

## Features That Matter

### Unlimited Everything (Actually Free)

Cal.com's free tier includes:
- Unlimited event types and connected calendars
- Email and SMS notifications
- 100+ app integrations (Zoom, Google Meet, Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Stripe and PayPal payment collection
- Mobile app and browser extension
- 1-click Calendly import

Calendly gates basic features like multiple event types behind paid plans. Cal.com doesn't.

### Self-Hosting for Total Control

This is the killer feature Calendly can't match. Cal.com is fully open source (AGPL license). You can:

- Self-host on your own infrastructure
- Audit every line of code
- Customize the booking experience
- Keep all data in-house

For healthcare, finance, or any compliance-heavy industry, this is non-negotiable. Docker deployment is straightforward:

```bash
docker pull calcom/cal.com
```

Or deploy via Railway, Coolify, or your preferred platform.

### Enterprise-Grade Compliance

Cal.com checks every compliance box:
- SOC 2 Type II
- HIPAA
- GDPR
- CCPA
- ISO 27001

The Organizations plan ($28/user/month) includes SAML SSO, SCIM provisioning, and custom subdomains. Calendly's enterprise pricing starts at "contact sales"—translation: expensive.

### Developer-First Architecture

Built with Next.js, TypeScript, and Prisma, Cal.com is designed for extensibility:
- Custom APIs on paid plans
- Webhook integrations
- App marketplace for extensions
- White-label options

Developers can build on top of Cal.com, not just use it.

## Team Scheduling That Works

Cal.com handles complex team scenarios:
- **Round-robin**: Distribute meetings across team members
- **Collective events**: Require multiple attendees to book together
- **Managed event types**: Admin-controlled booking templates
- **Routing forms**: Route bookings based on custom logic
- **Recurring events**: Set up repeating meeting slots

The Teams plan ($12/user/month) unlocks these features. Calendly requires Standard ($12) or higher for similar functionality.

## The AI Angle: Cal.ai

Cal.com recently launched Cal.ai—an AI scheduling assistant that handles the back-and-forth of finding meeting times via natural language. It's an optional add-on that positions Cal.com ahead of Calendly's feature curve.

## Who Should Switch?

**Choose Cal.com if:**
- You want unlimited free scheduling
- Data privacy and compliance matter
- You need self-hosting options
- You're building scheduling into your product
- You want to escape SaaS subscription creep

**Stick with Calendly if:**
- Your entire workflow is built around it
- You need specific enterprise integrations only they offer
- Switching costs exceed the savings (for now)

## Migration Is Painless

Cal.com includes a 1-click Calendly import. Your existing events, availability, and settings transfer automatically. You can test Cal.com alongside Calendly without disrupting your schedule.

## The Verdict

Cal.com isn't just a cheaper Calendly—it's a fundamentally better approach to scheduling infrastructure. The combination of open source, unlimited free features, and serious compliance credentials makes it the smart choice for individuals, teams, and enterprises.

Calendly built the category. Cal.com is building the future.

For new users, there's no contest. For existing Calendly users, the math gets compelling fast—especially as team size grows. And for anyone who believes in data sovereignty, Cal.com isn't just an alternative; it's the only real option.

**Bottom line:** Cal.com delivers more value at every price point, with the freedom to own your scheduling completely. In 2026, paying for Calendly is a choice, not a requirement.
